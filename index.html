The Throwbacks at the Old Castle Theater on September 6th, 2019

The Throwbacks are a band of youths that specialize in playing repertoire from decades of the past. Ranging from Doo Wop music including "Little Darlin'" by The Diamonds and "At The Hop" by Danny and The Juniors to classic rock with songs like "Don't Stop Me Now" by Queen, The Throwbacks play it all. The band works with singing tight harmonies and playing several instruments that work to replicate the vintage sounds of the past.

History

Back in 2015, Garrett Currier and Colton Crawford began playing music together. Garrett was the band's guitarist and pianist, while Colton was the bass player and drummer. They started calling themselves The Throwbacks and had one or two performances. As time passed, Colton's interests began to lean more towards sports. He eventually left the band to pursue his interest in sports. Later that year, Garrett participated in his school's talent show. He began playing music with friends Seth Button-Mosher, Owen Maroney, and Adrian Lindhardt during the intermission. With Adrian on piano, Seth on trumpet, Garrett on guitar, and Owen being introduced to drums for the first time, the new age of The Throwbacks had begun. At the end of the school year, four musicians along with others performed a song at the middle school graduation ceremony. During the following summer, Adrian moved away, but the other three continued to practice. At the end of the summer, the trio performed at a local music store to begin their publicity. The band entered high school that fall and was in the market for members. They soon met a guitarist named Rimmele Wood. In the winter of 2016, the group invited Rimmele to a rehearsal. He impressed all of them and was immedialty asked to join the band. He gladly accepted. For the next few months, the quartet began working on the band's early repertoire including "Hushabye" and "Rama Lama Ding Dong". In March of that year, the band joined by two others, performed Bob Marley's "Stir It Up" at an annual concert at their high school. The band was happy with where they were, but they were still missing something. That's when Gabe Schatz came into the picture. All the guys had known Gabe, but after they discovered his love for similar genres of music and musical talent, they knew he was a good addition to the band. At last the band was complete. In May of that year, the band debuted their name as The Throwbacks when they performed "Twist and Shout" at the high school's Beatles concert. The very next day, the Throwbacks played their first public performance at a local event and presented themselves as a band for hire. For the next few years, The Throwbacks continued to add repertoire, get their name out into the public, and perform frequently at events and concerts. The band's popularity grew very steadily. In the beginning of 2019, the group faced the facts that both Rimmele and Gabe were graduating from high school. Fortunately, Rimmele had decided he was taking a gap year and would be able to continue with the band, but Gabe was going on to college. They knew they would need to find someone to replace him. At a concert in March, the group had collaborated with their fellow student, John Cucciuni. They believed he could be a potential member for the band. Coincidentally, there was a performance coming up that Gabe wasn't going to be able to attend, so Garrett invited John to help them out at it. John gladly accepted the invitation and began to prepare. With only a few days of practice, John played a three hour performance with the band. While breaking down the equipment that night, the group discussed how they thought the show went and came to the conclusion that they thought John would be a wonderful addition for the band. They presented the idea to John, and he was more than excited to become a part of the band. That summer, The Throwbacks played several shows and made a big name for themselves. Though Gabe departed from the group that August, the band still continues to perform frequently and entertain people of all ages.

Band Members

Seth Button-Mosher

Seth Button-Mosher is one of the founding members of the band's official debut in the summer of 2016. Though everyone takes part in singing lead on the band's repertoire, Seth takes up lead vocals on the majority of the songs. His most well known songs are "The Twist", "Only You", and "Piano Man". In the beginnings of the band, he only sang one song which was "Twist and Shout", but he has taken a major step forward since then. He is the band's main trumpet player, and can be also found playing bass on several songs. Seth's favorite genre of music is classic rock and his favorite band is Queen. Seth also participates in various jazz ensembles and singing groups.

Rimmele Wood

Rimmele Wood has been with the band since December of 2016. He is the group's lead guitarist. Rimmele takes up lead vocals on several songs. A few of his most beloved songs to sing lead on are "Hallejulah" and "Bad Moon Rising". Rimmele's lead guitar is incredible and is spotlighted on songs like "Johnny B. Goode" and "Brown Eyed Girl". He also adds a folk music vibe to the band's genres. In addition to being the quintet's primary guitarist, he can be found playing bass guitar on a few songs. Rimmele's favorite types of music are pop and folk. His favorite artists are John Mayer and Paul Simon. Rimmele has written one song to date. On the side Rimmele takes part in local singing groups and theater productions.

Garrett Currier

Garrett Currier is one of the founding members of the band. He has been with the band since 2015. Garrett is the leader and manager of the band. Though he is mostly seen playing piano and drums, he plays guitar and bass on a few songs as well. He sings lead vocals on many of the earlier songs the band performs and his falsetto can be heard on the band's Doo Wop classics such as "The Lion Sleeps Tonight" and "Little Darlin". His favorite types of music are rock and roll, doo wop, and surf rock. Garrett's favorite band is The Beach Boys. Garrett has written several songs, and released his own album. He participates in various singing groups and ensembles.

Owen Maroney

Owen Maroney was one of the founding members in 2016 for the band's official debut. He is the band's primary drummer. He is also found on several songs playing bass. He is most known for his low voice he uses to sing lead on songs like "It Ain't My Fault" and "Ring of Fire". He also jumps in to play harmonica and auxiliary percussion. He loves classic rock of the 1970's and 1980's. His favorite band is Aerosmith. Owen has written one song. He particpates in several sports including football, basketball, and track and field. He also takes part in various ensembles.

John Cuccuini

John Cucciuni joined the band in April of 2019, a few months before Gabe was leaving so the band could maintain its usual five members. He is the youngest member of the group. John plays piano and bass, and auxiliary percussion. Along with joining the band, he brought his cover of "Lean On Me" to the lineup. He took up vocals on "Johnny B. Goode" and "Wonderful Tonight". John has written two songs along the lines of Doo Wop and Motown styles. John enjoys listening to classic rock and jazz. His favorite artist is Bill Withers and his favorite band is The Kinks. John enjoys particpating in theater productions and singing groups in his spare time.

Gabe Schatz(Former Member)

Gabe Schatz joined the band in April of 2017. He offered vocals, a second trumpet, bass guitar, and auxiliary percussion to the band's sound. He had a strong vocal presence in the group when he sang songs like "Escape" and "Johnny B. Goode". Due to his graduating from high school and going on to college the following fall, he sadly left the group. After Gabe departed from the band, he was replaced by John Cucciuni. He is a big fan of classic rock and enjoys listening to his favorite musical acts such as Fleetwood Mac and Billy Joel. Gabe enjoyes acting in theater productions.

Jackson Granger(Former Member)

Jackson Granger was in the band for a short span of time in the middle of the band's career. He has appeared as a concert guest and filled in for various members during performances on numerous ocassions. He had been offering aid and talent since the beginning, but officially joined at the end of 2018 to fill in for Garrett's temporary leave absence. He offered bass and guitar and a much heavier sound to the group's repertoire. Though his membership in the band was short lived, he still continues to assist the band when needed. Jackson's favorite genres of music are progressive rock and blues. He enjoys listening to Pink Floyd and Grateful Dead.

Adrian Lindhardt(Former Member)

Adrian Lindhardt served a short time in the group when the band made its return in 2016. He has written a number of songs. Adrian played piano for the group, but also knows how to play tenor saxophone as well. He only took part in a few performances, but his time with the group was cut short when he moved to California. Adrian still makes a guest appearance when he is back in town. His favorite styles of music are EDM and J-Pop, and his favorite artists are Panda Eyes and Sekai.

Colton Crawford(Former Member)

Colton Crawford was one of the founding members in the 2015 days of the band. Since his interests strayed away from music, he eventually left the band, but during his time he provided drums, bass guitar, and percussion. He also helped periodically on writing lyrics for songs. He has a deep love for baseball and other sports. Colton enjoys listening to country, rap, and classic rock.
